Current Travel Position

Egypt Travel Update

As of 2 April 2026, official travel advisories continue to distinguish Egypt from the active regional war zone. Egypt’s major tourist routes remain open, while specific border and high-risk areas remain restricted under government advisories.

Last reviewed: 2 April 2026 Based on official government advisories

What this means for travellers

  • Egypt is not being treated as an active war zone in current travel advisories. Advisories continue to issue country-specific guidance for Egypt rather than a blanket no-travel order for the country as a whole.
  • Main tourist destinations remain open. Current official advice continues to distinguish major travel areas from restricted zones such as North Sinai, remote border regions, and certain desert or military-sensitive areas.
  • Regional tension can still affect flights and border crossings. Travellers should continue to monitor airline schedules and official updates before departure.
  • Kaya travellers should still follow normal travel precautions. That includes checking the latest government advisory for your nationality before you travel.

Why we’re sharing this

We know some travellers may assume the whole region is affected in the same way. The official guidance does not say that. It separates Egypt’s main travel corridors from the specific areas where stronger restrictions apply.

This page is reviewed manually and should be checked again if official advisories change. It is meant as a travel-information summary, not a substitute for the latest government guidance for your passport country.
